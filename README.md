Supermarket Sales Performance Dashboard – Analysis Report
Overview
This project presents an interactive Excel dashboard designed to analyze supermarket sales performance across multiple cities, product lines, customer types, and payment methods. The dashboard consolidates key metrics into a single view, enabling quick insights into revenue distribution, customer behavior, and product trends. It is built using PivotTables, PivotCharts, slicers, and structured data modeling.

Dataset Summary
The dataset includes transactional sales records from three cities—Mandalay, Naypyitaw, and Yangon. Each record contains information on:
- City
- Product line
- Customer type (Member vs Normal)
- Gender
- Payment method
- Quantity
- Total sales
- Date of purchase
This structure allows for multi‑dimensional analysis of sales performance.

Dashboard Features
- Sales by City: Compares total revenue across Mandalay, Naypyitaw, and Yangon.
- Sales by Product Line: Highlights which product categories generate the highest sales.
- Sales by Month (Jan–Mar 2019): Shows short‑term sales trends and seasonality.
- Customer Type & Gender Analysis: Breaks down sales and quantity purchased by Member vs Normal customers, segmented by gender.
- Interactive Slicers:
- City
- Product Line
- Payment Method
- Gender
These allow users to filter the dashboard dynamically and explore patterns within specific segments.

Key Insights
1. City Performance
- Giza emerges as the top‑performing city in total revenue.
- Cairo follows closely, while Alex shows comparatively lower sales.
This suggests stronger customer demand or better operational performance in Giza.
2. Product Line Trends
- Food and Beverages is the highest‑grossing product line, indicating consistent demand and strong contribution to overall revenue.
- Other categories such as Electronic Accessories and Home & Lifestyle show moderate performance.
3. Monthly Sales Pattern
- Sales show noticeable variation across January, February, and March 2019, with a clear peak in one of the months.
- This pattern may reflect seasonal demand or promotional cycles.
4. Customer Type Behavior
- Members consistently purchase more items than Normal customers across all cities.
- Female Members are the most active segment, buying nearly double the quantity compared to other groups.
This highlights the effectiveness of the membership program and the importance of female shoppers in driving revenue.
5. Payment Preferences
- Sales are distributed across Cash, Credit Card, and E‑Wallet, with no single method dominating.
- This indicates a diverse customer base with varied payment habits.
