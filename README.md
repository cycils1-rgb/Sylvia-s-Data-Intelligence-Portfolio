## Project: Supermarket Sales Performance Dashboard – Analysis Report

### Overview
This project presents an **interactive Excel dashboard** designed to analyze **supermarket sales performance** across multiple cities, product lines, customer types, and payment methods.  
The dashboard consolidates key metrics into a single view, enabling **quick insights** into revenue distribution, customer behavior, and product trends.  

**Built using:** PivotTables, PivotCharts, interactive slicers, and structured data modeling.

---

### Dataset Summary
The dataset contains transactional sales records from **three cities**: Mandalay, Naypyitaw, and Yangon.  
Each record includes:
- **City**  
- **Product Line**  
- **Customer Type** (Member vs Normal)  
- **Gender**  
- **Payment Method**  
- **Quantity**  
- **Total Sales**  
- **Date of Purchase**  

This structure allows **multi-dimensional analysis** of sales performance.

---

### Dashboard Features
- **Sales by City:** Compares total revenue across Mandalay, Naypyitaw, and Yangon.  
- **Sales by Product Line:** Highlights highest-grossing product categories.  
- **Sales by Month (Jan–Mar 2019):** Shows short-term trends and seasonal patterns.  
- **Customer Type & Gender Analysis:** Breaks down sales and quantity by Members vs Normal customers, segmented by gender.  
- **Interactive Slicers:**  
  - City  
  - Product Line  
  - Payment Method  
  - Gender  

> Slicers allow users to filter the dashboard dynamically and explore patterns within specific segments.

---

### Key Insights

1. **City Performance**  
   - Giza emerges as the top-performing city in total revenue.  
   - Cairo follows closely; Alex shows comparatively lower sales.  
   - Suggests stronger customer demand or better operational performance in Giza.

2. **Product Line Trends**  
   - Food & Beverages is the highest-grossing category, indicating consistent demand.  
   - Electronic Accessories and Home & Lifestyle show moderate performance.

3. **Monthly Sales Patterns**  
   - Noticeable variation across January, February, and March 2019, with a peak in one month.  
   - May reflect **seasonal demand** or **promotional cycles**.

4. **Customer Type Behavior**  
   - Members consistently purchase more than Normal customers.  
   - Female Members are the most active segment, buying nearly double the quantity compared to others.  
   - Highlights the **effectiveness of membership programs** and the importance of female shoppers.

5. **Payment Preferences**  
   - Sales are distributed across Cash, Credit Card, and E-Wallet.  
   - Indicates a **diverse customer base with varied payment habits**.
